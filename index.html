Synopsis

We are trying to bridge that gap between online and in person classes. One common problem with the online/hybrid classes is the lack of the effactive use of a whiteboard. Normally, in-person class involves a whiteboard next to the notes projected on the screen. Now, however, in order accomodate in person students and virtual students at the same time, professors must share their screen virtually and project the screen in person. Because there are virtual students, this takes away being able to draw, explain, or elaborate using a whiteboard. This forces teachers to try to write using the trackpad of their computer or using a stylus with a bad angle, resulting in annotations that are less helpful than those on the whiteboard. Our research looks to develop a way for professors to comfortably use a whiteboard in a way that it useful to both in person students and online students.
